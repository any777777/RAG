4.5 Satellite Systems Using Small Earth Stations
Many satellite communication applications require only one or two telephone or data
channels, or the distribution of a television signal, while employing small, low-cost
earth stations. In such systems, earth station costs may range from a few hundred to
a few thousand dollars, making them attractive for domestic broadcasting, rural commu-
nications, and direct broadcast television.
The received power at an earth station depends on several parameters; however, from
the satellite perspective, only two parameters can be practically adjusted to support small
receiving antennas:
• Satellite transmitted power
• Satellite antenna beamwidth
Increasing transmitted power or narrowing the satellite antenna beam increases the
power density at the receiving earth station, allowing the use of smaller antennas.
Satellite Antenna Beamwidth Considerations
In domestic satellite systems, narrow beams are used to concentrate power over the
intended service region rather than illuminating the entire Earth.
• From geostationary orbit, the Earth subtends an angle of approximately 17◦.
• A microwave horn with an aperture only a few wavelengths in diameter can illumi-
nate the entire Earth.
• To achieve narrower coverage areas, larger aperture antennas are required.
Effect of Frequency
As the operating frequency increases:
• The antenna diameter measured in wavelengths increases for a fixed physical size.
• More directive (narrower) beams become feasible.
Example: At 4 GHz, a satellite antenna diameter of approximately 1.4 m is required to
produce a 4◦spot beam.
