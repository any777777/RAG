Section 1: Orbital Mechanics
P3 (10 pts) - Apogee and Perigee
Explain what is meant by apogee height and perigee height. The Cosmos 1675 satellite has an apogee height of
39,564 km and a perigee height of 657 km. Determine the semi-major axis and the eccentricity of its orbit. 
P4 (8 pts) - Circular Orbit Calculations
A satellite, orbiting in a circular orbit of an altitude of 500 km, carrying a transmitter operating at a frequency of
1 GHz. Calculate:
i. The orbital period in minutes.
ii. The orbital velocity in meters per second. 
P2.ii (3 pts) - Circular Orbit and Doppler Shift
A satellite is in a 850 km high circular orbit.
a) (1.5 pts) Determine the velocity of the satellite.
b) (1.5 pts) The satellite transmits at a carrier frequency of 2.4 GHz. Calculate the Doppler shift observed at the
ground station due to the satellite's motion.
