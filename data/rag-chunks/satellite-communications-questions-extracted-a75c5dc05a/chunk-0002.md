P4 (9 pts) - Link Parameters
A satellite located 41,000 km from an Earth station transmits a CW signal at a frequency of 10.7 GHz. The
satellite's transmitting antenna has a gain of 29.5 dB. The Earth station's receiving antenna has a diameter of 3.0
meters and an efficiency of 65%. The satellite transponder supplies 15 W of power to the transmitting antenna.
The receiver noise temperature is 1350 K, and the bandwidth is 200 Hz.
a) (1.5 pts) Calculate the free-space path loss in dB.
b) (1.5 pts) Determine the gain of the receiving antenna in dB.
c) (2 pts) Compute the received power at the Earth station in dBW and dBm.
d) (4 pts) Calculate the carrier-to-noise ratio (C/N) at the receiver input in dB. 
DBS-TV Link Budget
A DBS-TV receiver communicates with a Ku-band satellite transponder. The transponder output power is 160 W
and the receiver is located on -3 dB contour of the satellite antenna beam (i.e. at the edge of the coverage zone of
the satellite antenna). The satellite antenna gain is 34.3 dB and the receiving antenna gain is 34.9 dB. The link is
operational at 11.4 GHz having path length of 38,500 km. Miscellaneous losses of 0.4 dB for clear sky
attenuation and 0.55 dB for receiver antenna miss-pointing are allowed. At receiver, the antenna noise
temperature is set at 34 K in clear sky conditions and a LNA with 113 K noise temperature is used. The receiver
uses an IF bandwidth of 25 MHz.
a) (12 pts) Calculate the down link budget for Ku band DBS-TV receiver.
b) (3 pts) Assume that the minimum allowed C/N value is set at 7.4 dB. What link margin is achieved when the
above parameters are used?
