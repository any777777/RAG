A satellite system requires to have a minimum G/T given by: G/T = 41.5 + 20 log10(f / (4.2 × 109)) dBK-1.
Assume operation at 4.2 GHz with a terminal consisting of an antenna followed by a waveguide (physical
temperature 300 K) with 0.60 dB loss and a RF amplifier with a 1.6 dB noise figure and 14 dB gain. The RF
amplifier drives a mixer with a 7.5 dB noise figure and a 31 dB gain. After the mixer is an IF receiver with a 14
dB noise figure.
a) (2 pts) Calculate the required G/T.
b) (4 pts) Calculate the system noise temperature, referred to the antenna output port.
c) (3 pts) Calculate the receiving antenna gain in dB.
d) (3 pts) Calculate the system noise temperature, referred to the antenna output port, assuming that the antenna
noise temperature is 16 K.
e) (3 pts) Calculate the antenna diameter in meters necessary to meet the G/T specification. Assume that the
aperture efficiency is 60%.
