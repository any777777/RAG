Advanced Communication Systems
Tutorial 2 - Chapter 4 (Part1)
Department of Telecommunication Engineering — Yarmouk University
Problem 1
A satellite carrying an 11.7GHz continuous wave (CW) beacon transmitter is located
in geosynchronous orbit 38000km from an earth station. The beacon’s output power is
200mW, and it feeds an antenna with an 18.9dB gain toward the earth station. The earth
station receiving antenna is 12ft in diameter and has an aperture efficiency of 50 percent.
1. Calculate the satellite EIRP in W, dBW, and dBm.
2. Calculate the receiving antenna gain in dB.
3. Calculate the path loss in dB.
4. Calculate the received signal power in W, mW, and dBm.
Problem 2
If the overall system noise temperature of the earth station in Problem 1 is 1250K,
determine:
1. The earth station G/T in dBK−1.
2. The receiver noise power in a 100-Hz noise bandwidth in W and in dBm.
3. The receiver carrier-to-noise ratio in dB in a 100-Hz noise bandwidth.
Problem 3
An earth station receiving antenna for an INTELSAT V tracking beacon delivers –
119 dBm carrier power at the antenna’s output flange. The antenna noise temperature is
68K. Following the antenna is a waveguide with 1dB of loss and a physical temperature
of 295K. The output of the waveguide is connected to a GaAsFET amplifier with a U.S.
standard noise figure of 4dB and a gain of 25dB. Following the amplifier is a mixer with
a U.S. standard noise figure of 12dB. Calculate the following:
1. The GaAsFET amplifier noise temperature.
2. The mixer noise temperature.
3. The waveguide effective input noise temperature.
4. The overall noise temperature of the receiver, referred to the antenna output port.
5. The overall noise temperature of the receiver, referred to the GaAsFET input port.
6. The receiver carrier-to-noise ratio for a 200-Hz bandwidth.
