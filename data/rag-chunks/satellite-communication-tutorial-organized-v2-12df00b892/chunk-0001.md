Section 1: Kepler's Laws and Doppler Effect
Q1. (a) Kepler’s Third Law:
The square of the periodic time of orbit (T) is proportional to the cube of the mean
distance between two bodies (semimajor axis a). 
T² = (4π² a³) / μ
Where μ is the Earth's geocentric gravitational constant. 
(b) Doppler Effect Scenarios:
In a scenario with 4 passes of a satellite orbiting a rotating Earth with an Emergency
Locator Transmitter (ELT): 
Strongest Doppler Shift: Occurs in the second pass because the ELT is
closest to the satellite path underneath.
Positive Shift: When the satellite is approaching towards the ELT from the
South.
Negative Shift: When it passes away from ELT toward the North.
(c) Doppler Negligence:
In GEO (Geostationary) orbit, the Doppler effect can be ignored because the
satellite appears as a fixed station relative to Earth stations (orbital velocity matches
Earth's rotational velocity).

Section 2: Satellite Link & Antenna Calculations
Q2. Footprint and Gain:
Parameters: Range (r) = 42,000 km, Frequency (f) = 11 GHz, Footprint diameter
(F.P.) = 500 km, Satellite height (h) = 36,000 km, Efficiency (η) = 70%. 
Calculations:
1. λ = c / f = (3×10⁸) / (11×10⁹) = 0.02727 m
2. 3dB Beamwidth (θ): θ_3dB = 0.586°
3. Antenna Diameter (D): D = 75λ / θ_3dB ≈ 3.49 m
4. Gain (G): G = η(πD/λ)² = 113156.2 → 50.54 dB 
Section 3: Earth Station Visibility
Part II Q1. Elevation Angle & Visibility:
ES Location: 70° N, 50° W. SS Point: 5° E. 
Solution:
1. Central Angle (γ): cos γ = cos(L_e) cos(l_s - l_e) = cos(70°) cos(55°) → γ =
78.68°
2. Elevation Angle (El): Calculated as 4.86°.
3. Visibility Check: γ ≤ cos⁻¹(r_e / r_s). 78.68° ≤ 88.7°. Visibility is satisfied.
