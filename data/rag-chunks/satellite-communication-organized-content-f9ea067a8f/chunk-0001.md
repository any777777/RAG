Section 1: Geometric & Environmental Effects
1. The satellite azimuth angle is defined as:
The angle from the ES true North toward East up to the SS point. [Correct]
2. Propagation Delay is a problem for communication with GEO Satellites. It
can be reduced to minimum if:
The Elevation angle is increased up to 90°. [Correct]
3. Sun-Transit Outage of a satellite causes:
Significant increase in receiver noise. [Correct]
4. Eclipse of a satellite occurs when it enters:
In the shadow of the Earth. [Correct]
5. Doppler shift is observed to be strongest for:
LEO satellites. [Correct]
Section 2: Link Performance & Transponders
1. The (C/N) ratio is used in satellite link design, not (S/N) ratio because:
FM is used, where the signal amplitude is not the same as the carrier. [Correct]
2. To avoid Intermodulation Distortion caused by nonlinearity in the HPA, it is
recommended to:
Operate the HPA below the saturation point with a certain Back-off value. [Correct]

- DBS spacing: 9°.

Many Earth stations share the same satellite. [Correct]
