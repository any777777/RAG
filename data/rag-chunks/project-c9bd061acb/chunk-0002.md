1. Wang, Yang, Song, and Han, “Free Space Optical Communication for Inter-Satellite
Link: Architecture, Potentials and Trends,” 2024. Use this as the backbone for the ISL
architecture section, terminal workflow, and the three-part advantage framework: distance,
rate, and security.
https://www.researchgate.net/publication/378922022_Free_Space_Optical_Communication
_for_Inter-Satellite_Link_Architecture_Potentials_and_Trends
2. Terrasanta et al., “Photonic Integrated Circuits for Optical Satellite Links: A Review of
the Technology Status and Space Effects,” 2025. Use this for the “System Architecture
and Key Components” section, especially coherent transceivers, PICs, SWaP reduction,
and remaining integration barriers. https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/sat.1552
3. CCSDS 141.0-B-1, “Optical Communications Physical Layer,” 2019. Keep this as a
foundational standards reference for physical-layer architecture and cross-support framing.
It is older than the preferred window, but it is still the relevant systems standard.
https://ccsds.org/Pubs/141x0b1.pdf
4. CCSDS 142.0-B-1, “Optical Communications Coding and Synchronization,” 2019. Use
this when you discuss telemetry signaling, synchronization, LDPC, PN spreading, and 2-
PPM mapping. It gives your report standards-level credibility.
https://ccsds.org/Pubs/142x0b1.pdf
5. Sun et al., “End-to-End Performance Analysis of CCSDS O3K Optical Communication
System Under Atmospheric Turbulence and Pointing Errors,” 2025. This is one of the
best recent papers for connecting standards to realistic turbulence and pointing conditions.
Use it in the atmospheric effects and coding/interleaving discussion.
https://www.mdpi.com/2226-4310/12/10/869
6. Xu et al., “Satellite-to-ground optical communication systems under orbital
deviations and atmospheric turbulence,” 2025. Use this as a recent scientific anchor for
why space-to-ground optical links are harder than ISLs.
https://opg.optica.org/oe/fulltext.cfm?uri=oe-33-9-18912
