• During launch, the command system controls the firing of the apogee kick motor
(AKM), spin-up of spin-stabilized satellites, and deployment of solar panels and
antennas in three-axis stabilized satellites.
• The command structure incorporates safeguards to prevent unauthorized or acci-
dental changes to satellite operation.
• Encryption of command signals and responses is employed to ensure security of the
command system.
• After monitoring and analyzing telemetry data, appropriate commands are gener-
ated at the control terminal computer.
• Command words are transmitted to the satellite within a time-division multiplexed
(TDM) frame.
• After reception, the satellite checks the validity of the command word and sends it
back to the control station via the telemetry link for verification.
• If the command is verified correctly, an execute instruction is sent to the satellite.
• Although the complete command execution process may take 5 to 10 seconds, it
significantly reduces the risk of erroneous commands causing satellite malfunction.
3. Power Systems
The electrical power required to operate equipment onboard a communication satellite is
obtained primarily from solar cells, which convert incident sunlight into electrical energy.
The solar radiation intensity incident on a satellite averages approximately 1.4 kW/m2.
At the beginning of life (BOL), solar cells typically operate at an efficiency of 20–25%.
Over the satellite lifetime, usually assumed to be about 15 years, this efficiency degrades
to approximately 5–10% at end of life (EOL). Consequently, a large number of solar cells
connected in series–parallel arrays are required to meet the power demands of satellite
electronic systems, which often exceed 1–2 kW of primary power.
In spin-stabilized satellites, solar cells are generally mounted on cylindrical panels,
which may be extended after deployment to increase the effective exposure area. A cylin-
drical spin-stabilized satellite must carry a larger number of solar cells than an equivalent
three-axis stabilized satellite, since only about one-third of the cells are illuminated by
the Sun at any given time.
