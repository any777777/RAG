Multiple Access and Amplifier Backoff
When multiple carriers share a transponder:
• Nonlinear amplifiers generate intermodulation distortion
• Output power must be reduced below saturation
This reduction is known as output backoff.
Typical backoff values:
• FDMA systems: 3–7 dB
• TDMA systems: minimal or none
As a result, TDMA systems can achieve higher capacity than FDMA for the same
transponder power.
High-Power Amplifiers
At C-band and Ku-band, two main amplifier technologies are used:
• Traveling Wave Tube Amplifiers (TWTAs)
• Solid-State Power Amplifiers (SSPAs)
TWTAs provide high power but have limited lifetimes. SSPAs offer improved relia-
bility and are increasingly used, especially at 4 GHz and 11 GHz.

Advanced Communication Concepts
Further increases in satellite capacity are achieved through:
• Switched-beam antennas
• On-board baseband processing
• Data storage on the satellite
• Forward error correction
In switched-beam systems:
• Narrow, high-gain beams serve one earth station at a time
• Signals are time-multiplexed
• Satellite EIRP is greatly increased
Because the downlink usually limits performance, such techniques significantly in-
crease overall system capacity.
5. Spacecraft Antennas
Role of Antennas in Satellite Communication
Spacecraft antennas are a critical part of the communications subsystem. They determine:
• The geographical area covered by the satellite
• The gain and beamwidth of transmitted and received signals
• The achievable carrier-to-noise ratio at the earth station
In many satellite systems, antenna performance ultimately limits system capacity
more than transmitter power or bandwidth.
