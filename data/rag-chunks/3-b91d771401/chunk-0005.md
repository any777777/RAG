Three-Axis Body Stabilization
In three-axis body stabilization, the satellite is stabilized independently about the yaw,
roll, and pitch axes. This is achieved by mounting three momentum wheels along three
mutually perpendicular axes.
A momentum wheel is a high-speed rotating wheel enclosed in a sealed chamber.
Increasing or decreasing its speed alters the angular momentum and corrects attitude
deviations. Sensor data is transmitted to the earth station via telemetry, where it is
analyzed and used to generate commands that adjust the momentum wheel speeds. This
enables precise control about the roll axis (orbital plane), pitch axis (normal to the orbital
plane), and yaw axis.
Orbit Control System
A geostationary Earth orbit (GEO) satellite is subjected to several disturbing forces that
tend to accelerate it away from its assigned orbit. The primary function of the orbit
control system is to restore the satellite to its correct orbital position.

and changes in velocity. Momentum wheels cannot be used for orbit control since they
