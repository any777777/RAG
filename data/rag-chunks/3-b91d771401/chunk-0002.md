Need for Attitude and Orbit Control
Any change in the satellite’s spin axis leads to pointing errors between the satellite an-
tenna and the earth station antenna. This results in reduced received signal strength and
degradation of the carrier-to-noise ratio (C/N). Furthermore, external forces may alter
the satellite’s velocity and change the inclination of its orbital plane with respect to the
equatorial plane, causing the satellite to deviate from its geostationary position.
As a consequence, the earth station antenna would need continuous steering to main-
tain optimal signal reception. Therefore, maintaining correct attitude and orbit is essen-
tial for the reliable and optimized performance of satellite communication links.
Sensors
For attitude determination, satellites employ two primary types of sensors:
• Earth sensors
• Sun sensors
Earth Sensor
The Earth sensor is a passive infrared device operating in the wavelength range of 14–
16 µm. It detects infrared radiation emitted from the Earth’s horizon. Since space is
relatively cold and the Earth is warm, a sharp temperature contrast exists at the horizon.
Typically, two Earth sensors are positioned 5◦north and 5◦south of the satellite’s
spin axis. When the spin axis is correctly aligned, the outputs of the two sensors are in
phase; otherwise, they are out of phase. The phase difference pulses are transmitted to
the earth station, where they are used to determine the Earth aspect angle.
Sun Sensors
Sun sensors operate in the visible spectrum and employ photocells to detect solar radi-
ation. They have a fan-shaped field of view. Usually, two sun sensors are installed: one
parallel to the spin axis and another inclined at 35◦.
The pulses generated by the sun sensors are transmitted to the earth station to de-
termine the solar aspect angle. Data from both Earth and Sun sensors are analyzed by
ground-based computers to accurately determine the satellite orientation. Appropriate
commands are then sent to the satellite to fire rocket motors for axis correction.
