o Tracking of the Satellite: Is done by having the 
satellite transmit beacon signals to TT&C earth 
stations.
• Tracking is obviously important during the 
satellite launch. 
• Once it is on station, the position of a GEO 
satellite will tend to be shifted as a result of the 
various disturbing forces, described previously.
• Thus, it is necessary to track the satellite’s 
movement and send correction signals.

o Command: Command subsystem receives 
command signals from the earth station, often 
in response to telemetered  information.
•  It demodulates and decodes the command 
signals and routes them to the appropriate 
equipment needed to execute the necessary 
action such as:
Attitude changes
Transponders switched on and off.
 Antennas redirected, and 
Station-keeping maneuvers are carried out.
