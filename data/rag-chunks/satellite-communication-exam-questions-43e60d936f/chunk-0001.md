Part 2: Calculation Problems
Q1. [9 Points] - Circular Equatorial Orbit
An observation satellite is to be placed into a circular equatorial orbit such that it moves in the same direction as
the Earth's rotation. Using a synthetic aperture radar (SAR) system, the satellite stores data on surface barometric
pressure and other weather-related parameters as it flies overhead. These data are later played back to a
controlling Earth station after each trip around the world. The orbit is designed so that the satellite is directly
above the controlling Earth station, which is located on the equator, once every 4.5 hours. The controlling Earth
station's antenna is unable to operate below an elevation angle of 12° to the horizontal in any direction. Assume
the Earth's rotational period is exactly 24 hours.
1) [1.5 Points] Determine the satellite's angular velocity in radians per second.
2) [1.5 Points] Determine the orbital period in hours. 
Q5. [9 Points] - GEO Satellite Link Budget
A GEO satellite is part of a communication system that provides full Earth coverage of an FM modulated analog
TV channel. The uplink of this system has a C/N ratio of 21.5 dB and a C/I ratio of 20 dB. The downlink has a
C/I ratio of 22.5 dB.
