## Slide 19

5G - Key Features & Limitations

Enhanced Mobile Broadband (eMBB): Provides higher speeds and data capacity.
Ultra-Reliable and Low-Latency Communications (uRLLC): Ensures extremely reliable and fast communication, critical for applications like industrial automation.

## Slide 20

5G - Key Features & Limitations

Massive Machine-Type Communication (MMTC): Supports a huge number of connected devices, such as those in the Internet of Things (IoT).

AI Integration: While a major step forward, 5G does not deeply integrate AI into its core network architecture.

## Slide 23

6G - A Leap Forward in Capabilities

Very High Data Rates: Speeds up to 1 Tbps.
Extreme Energy Efficiency: The ability to support battery-free IoT devices.
Trusted Global Connectivity: Seamless and secure global coverage.

## Slide 24

6G - Forward in Capabilities

Massive Low-Latency Control: Less than 1 msec end-to-end latency.
Expanded Spectrum: Utilization of very broad frequency bands (e.g., 73GHz-140GHz and 1THz-3THz).

## Slide 25

6G - A Leap Forward in Capabilities

Ubiquitous Coverage: Integration of terrestrial wireless with satellite systems for a truly global network.
Ubiquitous Coverage," it refers to a network that provides connectivity in virtually all locations, leaving no dead zones.

## Slide 26

6G - A Leap Forward in Capabilities

Connected Intelligence: A built-in network hierarchy with machine learning and AI capabilities.

## Slide 27

6G Innovations Beyond 5G

Computation Oriented Communications (CoC): Enables distributed and in-network computation, supporting functionalities like federated learning and edge intelligence. Instead of just Quality of Service, it focuses on achieving a certain computational accuracy.

## Slide 28

6G Innovations Beyond 5G

6G services will be more adaptive to various contexts, including network conditions, physical environments, and social networks.

## Slide 29

6G Innovations Beyond 5G

Designed to support low-latency communication in extreme or emergency events where network conditions are volatile, unlike 5G which relies on redundant resources.
